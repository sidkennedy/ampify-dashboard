# CLINICAL PARTNERSHIP & REFERRAL AGREEMENT

> **⚠️ IMPORTANT — NOT LEGAL ADVICE.** This is a plain-language **draft template** to align both sides before a lawyer finalizes it. It is **not** a substitute for legal counsel. Before either party signs, have it reviewed by a qualified attorney in your state — especially the equity, IP, tax, and healthcare-compliance sections. Bracketed items [like this] need to be filled in or confirmed.

**This Clinical Partnership & Referral Agreement** ("Agreement") is entered into as of **[Effective Date]** by and between:

- **[Company legal name — e.g., Ampify LLC]**, [a [State] limited liability company] / [Sidney Kennedy, an individual, doing business as Ampify] (the "**Company**"); and
- **[Nicole Full Legal Name]**, an individual ("**Partner**").

The Company and Partner are each a "Party" and together the "Parties."

## Recitals

A. The Company has developed and owns a software platform that automates insurance eligibility and benefits verification for audiology and hearing-care practices (the "**Platform**").

B. Partner is an experienced audiology practice owner who originated the concept, contributes clinical and insurance domain expertise, and has relationships with numerous private-practice owners.

C. The Parties wish to formalize a partnership under which Partner supports the Platform's development and refers new practices, in exchange for the compensation described below.

The Parties agree as follows:

---

## 1. Definitions

1.1 **"Referred Practice"** — any audiology or hearing-care practice that becomes a paying customer of the Company and that (a) Partner introduced to the Company, and (b) is recorded as a Partner referral by the Company at or before onboarding.

1.2 **"Net Subscription Revenue"** — subscription fees actually received by the Company from a Referred Practice, less refunds, chargebacks, and payment-processing fees. It excludes taxes and any third-party pass-through costs.

1.3 **"Confidential Information"** — non-public business, technical, financial, customer, and product information disclosed by one Party to the other.

## 2. Partner Role

2.1 Partner is appointed the Company's **Clinical Partner** and will, on a continuing basis: (a) provide audiology/insurance domain expertise and verification scenarios; (b) act as the Company's founding reference practice; and (c) introduce and refer prospective practices.

2.2 **Independent contractor.** Partner is an independent contractor, not an employee, agent, or joint venturer of the Company. Partner has no authority to bind the Company.

2.3 **Non-exclusive effort; exclusive clinical role.** Partner sets her own schedule and methods. During the Term, Partner will serve as the Company's exclusive clinical partner within audiology and will not provide equivalent services to, or hold an interest in, a competing verification product (see Section 8).

## 3. Founding-Practice Benefit

3.1 Partner's practice, **[Preferred Audiology Care / Syracuse Hearing Solutions]**, receives use of the Platform **free of subscription charges for the life of this Agreement**, subject to fair-use and applicable third-party pass-through costs (if any).

## 4. Referral Revenue Share

4.1 **Rate.** The Company will pay Partner **twenty percent (20%) of Net Subscription Revenue** from each Referred Practice.

4.2 **Duration.** The revenue share is paid for as long as the Referred Practice remains an active paying customer, including after termination of this Agreement, subject to Section 9.4.

4.3 **Payment.** Paid **monthly**, within [30] days after the end of each calendar month, for revenue collected in that month.

4.4 **Statements & audit.** Each payment is accompanied by a statement of Referred Practices and amounts. Partner may, no more than [once per year] on reasonable notice, review records relevant to the calculation.

4.5 **Attribution.** A practice is credited to Partner only if recorded as a Partner referral at or before onboarding. Disputed attribution is resolved in good faith; the Company's onboarding records control absent clear evidence otherwise.

## 5. Equity

5.1 **Grant.** The Company will issue to Partner equity equal to **ten percent (10%)** of the Company on a [fully-diluted] basis as of the Effective Date, on the terms of the Company's equity/operating documents ("Equity").

5.2 **Vesting.** The Equity vests over **three (3) years**, with a **one-year cliff** (25% after 12 months, then monthly/quarterly thereafter), contingent on Partner's continued performance under this Agreement.

5.3 **Documentation.** The Equity is subject to separate equity documents (e.g., the Company's operating agreement / a grant or vesting agreement), which control in case of conflict. Tax treatment is Partner's responsibility; Partner should seek tax advice (including any [83(b)] considerations).

5.4 **Unvested equity** is forfeited if this Agreement terminates, except as the Parties agree in writing.

## 6. Company Responsibilities

6.1 The Company builds, hosts, maintains, supports, and bills for the Platform; funds infrastructure and compliance; and sets pricing and product direction.

## 7. Confidentiality

7.1 Each Party will keep the other's Confidential Information confidential, use it only for this partnership, and protect it with reasonable care. Obligations survive termination for [3] years. Standard exclusions apply (information that is public, already known, independently developed, or rightfully received from a third party).

## 8. Intellectual Property & Non-Circumvention

8.1 **Company ownership.** The Company solely and exclusively owns all right, title, and interest in the Platform, including all software, technology, data, know-how, processes, and improvements, whether or not derived from Partner's input. Nothing transfers IP to Partner except the Equity in Section 5.

8.2 **License of contributions.** Partner grants the Company a perpetual, irrevocable, royalty-free license to use the clinical/insurance expertise, scenarios, and feedback she contributes, in the Platform and the business.

8.3 **Non-circumvention / non-compete.** During the Term and for **[12] months** after, Partner will not (a) build, operate, advise, or hold a material interest in a product that competes with the Platform in audiology verification; (b) solicit Referred Practices to leave the Company; or (c) disclose the Company's Confidential Information or methods to a competitor.

## 9. Term & Termination

9.1 **Term.** Begins on the Effective Date and continues until terminated.

9.2 **Termination for convenience.** Either Party may terminate on **[60] days'** written notice.

9.3 **Termination for cause.** Either Party may terminate immediately for the other's material breach not cured within [30] days of notice.

9.4 **Survival.** On termination: (a) revenue share continues on practices referred **before** termination for as long as they remain customers [, for up to [—] months / indefinitely — confirm]; (b) vested Equity is retained per Section 5 and the equity documents; (c) Sections 7, 8, 9.4, and 10 survive.

## 10. General

10.1 **Governing law.** [State], without regard to conflict-of-laws rules.

10.2 **Dispute resolution.** The Parties will first attempt good-faith resolution; unresolved disputes go to [mediation/arbitration in [County, State]] / [the courts of [County, State]].

10.3 **Entire agreement.** This Agreement (with the equity documents) is the entire agreement and supersedes prior discussions. Amendments must be in writing and signed.

10.4 **Assignment.** Neither Party may assign without the other's consent, except the Company may assign in a financing, merger, or sale of substantially all its assets.

10.5 **No employment / no partnership entity.** This Agreement does not create an employment relationship or a separate legal partnership entity; "partner" is used in its general business sense.

10.6 **Severability; waiver.** If any provision is unenforceable, the rest remains in effect. No waiver is implied by delay.
