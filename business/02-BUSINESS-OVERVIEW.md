# Ampify — Business Overview

*Prepared for partnership discussion · June 2026 · Confidential*

> This document is shared in confidence to support our partnership conversation. Figures are conservative, good-faith estimates and will be refined as we grow.

---

## 1. The opportunity in one paragraph

Every audiology practice has to verify a patient's insurance benefits before care — and today that means a biller spending **20–40 minutes per patient** on hold with payers, or skipping the check and eating denials later. **Ampify is an AI platform that performs these verifications automatically and returns clean, structured benefit details to the practice in minutes** — no hold time, no missed checks, far fewer denials. We've built it and proven it against real payers using a live audiology practice (Preferred Audiology Care) as our design partner.

---

## 2. The problem

- A typical practice runs **dozens of eligibility/benefit checks per day**.
- Each manual verification ties up a biller for **20–40 minutes**, much of it on hold.
- At a loaded staff cost of ~$25–35/hour, that's **$10–20 of labor per verification** — before counting the revenue lost to denied or unverified claims.
- Verification is the single most repetitive, time-draining task in the billing office, and it scales linearly with patient volume.

## 3. The solution

Ampify automates the entire verification: the practice enters the patient and payer, and the platform obtains active coverage, deductible/out-of-pocket status, cost-share, prior-authorization flags, and audiology-specific benefits — then returns it in a clean, consistent format the biller can act on immediately. What took 30 minutes on hold becomes a few minutes, hands-off.

**Status:** the core platform is built and has been validated against multiple major payers (including national and regional carriers) using real patient data. We're now refining the experience and preparing to onboard additional practices.

## 4. Why now / why us

- The technology to do this reliably only matured recently.
- **Domain expertise is the moat.** Generic automation fails here; getting the *right* audiology codes, scenarios, and benefit nuances correct is what makes the output trustworthy. That expertise is exactly what this partnership brings.
- We have a **working product and a real reference clinic** — most competitors have neither.

---

## 5. Market

- There are **thousands of independent audiology and hearing-care practices** in the U.S., nearly all facing the same verification burden.
- Our initial wedge is **private-practice audiology** — underserved by big-vendor software and highly networked (owners talk to each other and refer tools they trust).
- **Nicole's network of hundreds of private-practice owners is a direct, warm channel into exactly this market** — the single biggest accelerant to scale.

---

## 6. Business model & pricing

Simple, predictable **monthly subscription per practice**, sized to verification volume:

| Plan | Monthly | Included verifications/mo | Best for |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Starter** | $399 | ~75 | Solo / low-volume |
| **Growth** | $899 | ~200 | Established practice |
| **Practice** | $1,499 | ~400 | High-volume / multi-provider |

- Overage billed per verification above the plan cap.
- Pricing sits **well below the labor it replaces** (~$10–20/verification manual), so the value case for a practice is immediate and obvious.
- Subscriptions create **predictable, recurring revenue** and high retention (verification is mission-critical and used daily).

---

## 7. Unit economics (conservative)

We deliver verifications at a low, largely automated cost; the main standing costs are compliance and infrastructure, which are **mostly fixed** and absorbed quickly as practices are added.

| | Conservative estimate |
|---|---|
| Avg revenue per practice | **~$1,000 / month** |
| Avg cost to deliver (per practice, all-in) | **~$450 / month** |
| **Contribution per practice** | **~$550 / month (~55%)** |
| Fixed platform/compliance/infra | ~$1,300 / month base (scales slowly) |

The business has **strong operating leverage**: each new practice adds revenue at high contribution margin while fixed costs barely move. Margins start moderate (fixed costs spread over few clinics) and **expand toward 65–70%+ as the network grows.**
