# BUSINESS ASSOCIATE AGREEMENT (BAA)

> **⚠️ NOT LEGAL ADVICE — DRAFT TEMPLATE.** HIPAA BAAs have legally required elements; this covers the standard ones but **must be reviewed and finalized by a healthcare attorney** before use. Bracketed items [like this] need confirming.

This Business Associate Agreement ("**BAA**") is entered into between the **Clinic** ("**Covered Entity**") and **[Ampify LLC]** ("**Business Associate**"), and is incorporated into the Clinic Services Agreement. It is **accepted by the Clinic at onboarding** (same checkbox/acceptance as the Services Agreement). It governs Business Associate's handling of Protected Health Information ("**PHI**") under HIPAA (45 CFR Parts 160 & 164) and the HITECH Act.

## 1. Definitions
Terms used but not defined here have the meanings in HIPAA. "PHI" means Protected Health Information that Business Associate creates, receives, maintains, or transmits **on behalf of the Covered Entity** in providing the Services.

## 2. Permitted uses & disclosures
Business Associate may use and disclose PHI **only**: (a) to perform the Services and as permitted by the Services Agreement; (b) as Required by Law; (c) for Business Associate's proper management and administration and to carry out its legal responsibilities, provided disclosures are Required by Law or the recipient is bound to protect the PHI and to notify Business Associate of breaches; and (d) to de-identify PHI in accordance with 45 CFR 164.514(a)-(c). Business Associate will not use or disclose PHI other than as permitted by this BAA or as would violate HIPAA if done by the Covered Entity.

## 3. Minimum necessary
Business Associate will limit its use, disclosure, and request of PHI to the **minimum necessary** to accomplish the intended purpose.

## 4. Safeguards
Business Associate will implement and maintain administrative, physical, and technical **safeguards** that reasonably and appropriately protect the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of PHI, and will comply with the HIPAA **Security Rule** with respect to electronic PHI (ePHI).

## 5. Subcontractors
Business Associate will ensure that any **subcontractors** that create, receive, maintain, or transmit PHI on its behalf agree in writing to **restrictions and conditions at least as protective** as those in this BAA (flow-down BAAs). *(Business Associate's subcontractors include its clearinghouse, voice/telephony provider, and cloud/database provider; Business Associate maintains BAAs with each.)*

## 6. Reporting & breach notification
Business Associate will report to the Covered Entity, without unreasonable delay and no later than **[30] days** after discovery: (a) any use or disclosure of PHI not permitted by this BAA; (b) any **Security Incident**; and (c) any **Breach** of Unsecured PHI. For a Breach, the report will include the information required by 45 CFR 164.410 to the extent known. Business Associate will reasonably cooperate with the Covered Entity's breach-response obligations.

## 7. Individual rights
To the extent Business Associate holds PHI in a Designated Record Set, it will: (a) make PHI available to enable the Covered Entity to respond to **access** requests (45 CFR 164.524); (b) make PHI available for **amendment** and incorporate amendments (164.526); and (c) provide information needed for an **accounting of disclosures** (164.528). Business Associate will forward to the Covered Entity any such requests it receives directly.

## 8. Access by HHS
Business Associate will make its internal practices, books, and records relating to the use and disclosure of PHI available to the **Secretary of HHS** for purposes of determining compliance.

## 9. Return or destruction
Upon termination, Business Associate will, if feasible, **return or destroy** all PHI it maintains on the Covered Entity's behalf and retain no copies. If return/destruction is not feasible, Business Associate will extend the protections of this BAA to such PHI and limit further use/disclosure to the reasons that make return/destruction infeasible.

## 10. Term & termination
This BAA is effective on acceptance and continues until all PHI is returned or destroyed. The Covered Entity may terminate the Services Agreement if Business Associate materially breaches this BAA and fails to cure within **[30] days** of notice.

## 11. Mitigation & general
Business Associate will mitigate, to the extent practicable, any harmful effect of a use or disclosure of PHI in violation of this BAA. This BAA will be interpreted to permit compliance with HIPAA; in the event of conflict between this BAA and the Services Agreement regarding PHI, this BAA controls. Amendments will be in writing as needed to comply with changes in law.

---

## Acceptance
Accepted by the Clinic at onboarding via the **"I have read and agree"** checkbox covering the Clinic Services Agreement and this BAA. Ampify records the accepting individual's name, title, date/time, and version as the electronic signature.

> Reminder: a healthcare attorney should review and finalize this BAA before it is used with real clinics, and confirm BAAs are in place with all PHI-handling subcontractors (clearinghouse, voice provider, cloud/database provider).
