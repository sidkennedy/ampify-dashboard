## Appendix: How your equity works (plain English)

Your **20% referral share** and your **10% equity** are two separate things:

- **The 20% referral share is income** — cash paid to you every month on the practices you bring in.
- **The 10% equity is ownership** — your stake in the company itself, which pays off through profit distributions and, most significantly, if the company is ever sold.

**What the 10% gives you.** A 10% share of the company's value. For example, if the company were ever sold for $2,000,000, your stake would be worth roughly $200,000; at $5,000,000, roughly $500,000. To keep day-to-day decisions simple, the equity is **non-voting** — you share fully in the financial upside as an owner, while operational decisions stay streamlined.

**Vesting — you earn it over 3 years.** Rather than receiving all 10% on day one, you earn it over three years of continued partnership. If the partnership were to end, you keep the portion you've already earned, and the rest returns to the company. This is standard and protects both sides.

**The 1-year cliff.** Nothing vests during your first 12 months. On your **one-year anniversary, a full third of the grant vests at once**, and the remainder then accrues monthly until you're fully vested at year three:

| Time as Partner | Equity earned (vested) |
|---|---|
| 0–11 months | 0% |
| **12 months (1-year mark)** | **3.33%** (first third locks in) |
| 18 months | ~5.0% |
| 24 months | ~6.67% |
| **36 months** | **10.0% — fully earned** |

Once vested, it's yours to keep — even if your day-to-day involvement changes later.

**A couple of practicalities.** The equity is issued through the company's formal equity documents (which control the details), and is typically structured as a tax-efficient "profits interest." You should speak with your own accountant about the tax treatment (including a possible 83(b) election). If the company later raises outside investment, all owners — including both of us — are diluted proportionally; your stake is 10% as of the grant date.
